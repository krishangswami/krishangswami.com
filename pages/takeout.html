DESCRIPTION
This is a class project. The aim of this project was to design a take-out box for food.



As a class project, the design had many restrictions:
"You may use up to 3 separate pieces of E-flute cardboard. All pieces must be able to come from a single 18 x 24" sheet. No fasteners (nails, screws, etc.) or glue may be used. Tabs and slots should be used to connect sections within the design."

The many prototypes I made explored origami folds and different tabs. The process of iterating new designs and versions incrementally made my design better.

PROCESS

I found origami folds and designs too complex for the E-flute cardboard, therefore, the designs developed further had simpler folds and no curves where the cardboard could crack.

For joining sides and different pieces together I used tabs and supports from here.

My final design utilized tabs and was made from one piece of cardboard. It had a triangular shape and a flat back that added stability, strength and grip to the box.
